Project Background

Al Sadd Global Trade Ltd, incorporated in Saudi Arabia in 2012, is a supplier of agricultural primary products. Our company ran a strategic discount campaign from 2023-2024 across different cities in the country. I'm partnering with the marketing team to extract insights and offer recommendations on the outcome of the discount campaign and improve performance across sales, product and marketing teams.

Executive Summary

In the 2023–2024 discount campaign, Al Sadd Global Trade Ltd generated 630K in coffee bean total sales across 100 customers in 10 cities in the country, with 97% participation driving 98% of total sales underscoring extremely elastic demand for promotions. Seasonality was pronounced: monthly sales peaked in June (57,208) and March (56,519), and troughed in April (47,882) and October (47,432), maybe a reflection of cultural and climatic consumption cycles. On a daily basis, revenue held steady between 10k–25k. By product types, Colombian beans led with roughly 22% of the portfolio share, followed close by Costa Rican also 22%, Ethiopian 21%, Guatemalan 16%, and Brazilian with also 16%. Geographically, Hail recorded the highest overall sale (77k), whereas Tabuk lagged (51k), highlighting prime targets for hyper local activations/promotions.

Recommendations

  • Strategic Discounting
  • Inventory Prioritization
  • Geo Targeting
  • Seasonal Planning
  • Mobile app

    • Strategic Discounting

    • Deploy 15–20% flash promotions during April and October.
    • Measure incremental lift by comparing post campaign revenue against the 3 month moving average baseline.

    Inventory Prioritization

    • Allocate 40% of stocking capacity to Colombian and Costa Rican beans, based on their 2023–2024 sell through rates.
    • Implement dynamic reorder points via rolling 3 month velocity to minimize stockouts.

    Geo focused Activation

    • Launch targeted digital ad campaigns and in store sampling in Tabuk and Abha.
    • Aim for a 10% Quarter on Quarter sales uplift, tracked via ZIP code level dashboards.

    Mobile App & Loyalty

    • Develop a mobile app with purchase history, personalized push offers, and a tiered membership program. Target a 15% uplift in repeat purchase rate and a 20% reduction in acquisition cost within 6 months.

    Clarifying Questions, Assumptions and Caveats

    Clarifying Questions

    • Discount Criteria Variance: What business rules determined each customer’s discount rate (e.g, volume tiers, loyalty status, first time purchase)?
    • Customer ID Duplication: Does each Customer ID represent a single individual with multi location orders, or is the ID reused per regional module?
    • New vs Returning Customer Tracking: How does our system distinguish between first time buyers and repeat purchasers via signup dates, or order history, to know if the campaign attracted new customers or just returning customers?
    • Pre Campaign Profit Comparison: Can we access gross margin data for the same customers in the year preceding the discount campaign, to calculate incremental profit and ROI of the discount?

    Assumptions

    I assumed every customer id as one customer although same customer id had appeared in different other cities. This may overestimate unique customer counts and obscure geographic loyalty. I propose we use emails or phone numbers to dedupe IDs across regions.

    Caveats

    We need more on customer demographics (e.g., age, gender and time of purchases) than just geographical locations. Omitting demographics could bias segment analysis (e.g., age driven preferences) and limit personalization strategies. If 70% of sales came from one demographic, lacking that data means i cannot validate targeted campaigns.

    Conclusion

    This analysis provides clear insights into customer behavior, seasonal fluctuations and product preferences. This data underscores the importance of promotional strategies and regional analysis in optimizing sales performance.
